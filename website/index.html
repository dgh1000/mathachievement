Let me extend a welcome to all students who would like to gain new capabilities and confidence at math. This site presents essays and ideas that can help you, wehther math is a struggle or you naturally excel at it.

If you excel at math, I recommend you begin at this page.

If you struggle with math, continue reading. First, let me acknowledge the situation. Math really is difficult and seems disconnected from anything else in life that you may care about. If you are discouraged that you have to take many math classes in order to graduate, that is understandable.

Your brain can do math. I know that you have the brain cells to do it. Your brain is a tool. When you use a tool like a screwdriver, you have to use it in the right way. You wouldn't hold a screwdriver by the shaft and turn the screw with the handle: that wouldn't work very well. It may be that you aren't using your brain very effectively for math right now.

First, read the journey which presents math problem solving as navigating your way through an unfamiliar territory to reach your home. You already know a lot about navigating through the world, like finding a new restaurant in a new neighborhood. We can take that perspective and harness it for math.

Second, you probably know a lot about manipulating the world. You know how to rearrange things to achieve a goal, like straightening up a room. As a child you learned to build things out of blocks. You probably feel some sense of power and control in the "physical universe," the world of objects. Math is a "universe" that also it contains objects which you can rearrange and you can build with. Read make it so to gain confidence in manipulating the world of math: if you wish it to be done, then in the words of Captain Picard, you can "make it so."
